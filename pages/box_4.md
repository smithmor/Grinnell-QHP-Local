**Queer Cultures Week Poster** <br>
Unknown, 2014

A poster displaying the program for “Queer Cultures Week” in 2014, this flier features wide-ranging programming, including ones by Safe, Sane, and Consensual, a student group founded in 2013 to provide education about “BDSM, kink, fetish, and other aspects of sexuality that are marginalized, stigmatized, or otherwise erased.” It also features a lecture by Lydia Brown, a prominent autistic disability rights activist, speaker, and civil rights lawyer on disability and queerness.

**Scarlet and Black, “Making the Move”** <br>
Leslie Boyadjian and Lauren Standifer, 2004

**Scarlet and Black, “SRC finds Home in Younker”** <br>
Mario Macias, 2007

The Stonewall Resource Center (SRC) made its first move around campus in 2004 as the old Younker Medical Center, located near where the JRC now stands, was being prepared for demolition. It was moved to the pit of Loose Hall for the duration of the construction of the JRC, despite concerns that there would not be enough space for the multi-room space that contained a “computer room, kitchen, meeting room, library, bathroom, and social space” in the Younker Medical Center. Following the construction of the JRC in 2007, it was briefly moved into the new campus center, where it was divided between two different floors, before taking up its current residence in the former Younker Hall Smoking Lounge.
